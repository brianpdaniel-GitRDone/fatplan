Daily Nutrition Targets

1,800 calories is the floor — not 1,600. At 60 with daily training, going below this accelerates muscle loss and cortisol elevation, both of which stall fat loss. Hold this number unless advised otherwise.

The protein target is not negotiable. Every gram under 185 increases muscle loss risk. At 60, you lose muscle 2–3× faster than a 30yr old in a deficit. The whey shake + 5 whole eggs/day are what close the gap.

10–12 Week Roadmap from 198 lbs

Official start: July 12, 2026. Target: 13 lbs in 10–12 weeks (1.3 lbs/week average). The last 3–4 lbs will slow — plan for that now instead of being surprised by it.

Weeks 1–3
Adaptation
~194–196 lbs
Expect 2–3 lbs fast (water + glycogen depletion). Do not count this as fat loss. Primary goal: nail the Sunday prep system and build the walk habit. The habit is the result.
Weeks 4–7
True fat loss
~190–193 lbs
Real fat loss begins: 1–1.5 lbs/week. Energy may dip weeks 4–5 — this is metabolic adaptation, not a sign the plan is broken. Add creatine now if you haven't started. Increase workout volume per the progression.
Weeks 8–10
Stall zone
~187–190 lbs
A stall at weeks 8–10 is normal physiology. The body adapts to the deficit. Do NOT cut calories. Response: add a 20-min post-dinner walk 3 nights per week. Check sleep — under 6.5 hours halts fat loss measurably at 60.
Weeks 11–12
Final stretch
~185–187 lbs
Last 3–4 lbs is the hardest. Body fights back near goal weight. Hold protein target. Consider adding one refeed day per week at maintenance calories (+400 cal carbs only) to reset leptin.
Week 13+
Maintenance
185 lbs
Add 200–300 cal/day back (carbs only, not fat or protein). Keep the exact same meal structure — the Sunday prep system is now a lifestyle, not a diet. Hold weight within 3 lbs for 8 weeks to reset your physiological set-point at 185.
The stall at weeks 8–10 is the most common failure point. People cut calories, feel worse, performance drops, they quit. The correct response is always activity first, calories never.

Stall Protocol — In Order

If weight stops moving for 10+ consecutive days, work through these steps in order. Do not skip ahead. Most stalls resolve at step 1 or 2.

1
Add a 20-min post-dinner walk 3 nights per week. Do this for 10 full days before concluding it didn't work. This is a 200–300 cal/day increase in expenditure without touching the diet.
2
Audit actual food intake for 3 days — weigh everything. Eyeballing portions drifts 20–30% high over time. The most common cause of stalls is calorie creep, not metabolic adaptation.
3
Check sleep. Less than 6.5 hours of sleep elevates cortisol, suppresses fat oxidation, and measurably halts fat loss. Magnesium glycinate at 400mg is the first intervention. No screens after 9pm.
4
Add one refeed day per week. Eat at maintenance calories (~2,200–2,400) adding carbs only — not fat or protein. This resets leptin, the hormone that signals your brain to allow fat burning. Do for 2 weeks and reassess.
5
Reassess with advisor only after steps 1–4 have been tried for a minimum of 2 weeks each. Changing the diet is always the last resort, not the first.
What NOT to do when you stall: Cut calories below 1,800. Add more exercise sessions. Skip the shake. These responses feel logical but they accelerate metabolic adaptation, increase muscle loss, and make the stall worse.
