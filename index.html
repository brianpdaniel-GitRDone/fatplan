Daily Nutrition Targets

1,800 calories is the floor — not 1,600. At 60 with daily training, going below this accelerates muscle loss and cortisol elevation, both of which stall fat loss. Hold this number unless advised otherwise.

The protein target is not negotiable. Every gram under 185 increases muscle loss risk. At 60, you lose muscle 2–3× faster than a 30yr old in a deficit. The whey shake + 5 whole eggs/day are what close the gap.

The 3pm shake is your evening hunger defense. Placed deliberately to bridge lunch and dinner, it eliminates the salty-snack urge before it starts. If you find yourself hungry after dinner, move the shake 30 minutes later — don't add food.

90–120 Day Roadmap

Target: 20 lbs in 15–17 weeks (1.3 lbs/week average). The last 5 lbs will take as long as the first 15 — plan for that now instead of being surprised by it.

Weeks 1–3
Adaptation
~200–202 lbs
Expect 3–5 lbs fast (water + glycogen depletion). Do not get excited — this is not fat. Primary goal: nail the Sunday prep system and build the walk habit. The habit is the result.
Weeks 4–8
True fat loss
~194–198 lbs
Real fat loss begins: 1–1.5 lbs/week. Energy may dip weeks 4–5 — this is metabolic adaptation, not a sign the plan is broken. Add creatine now if you haven't started. Increase workout volume per the progression.
Weeks 9–12
Stall zone
~190–194 lbs
A stall at weeks 9–10 is normal physiology. The body adapts to the deficit. Do NOT cut calories. Response: add a 20-min post-dinner walk 3 nights per week. Check sleep — under 6.5 hours halts fat loss measurably at 60.
Weeks 13–17
Final stretch
~185–188 lbs
Last 5–8 lbs is the hardest. Body fights back near goal weight. Hold protein target. Consider adding one refeed day per week at maintenance calories (+400 cal carbs only) to reset leptin and keep metabolism from downregulating further.
Week 18+
Maintenance
185 lbs
Add 200–300 cal/day back (carbs only, not fat or protein). Keep the exact same meal structure — the Sunday prep system is now a lifestyle, not a diet. Hold weight within 3 lbs for 8 weeks to reset your physiological set-point at 185.
The stall at weeks 9–10 is the most common failure point. People cut calories, feel worse, performance drops, they quit. The correct response is always activity first, calories never.

Supplement Protocol

Four supplements only. Everything else is noise and expense. These are the ones with 20+ years of evidence at your age and goal.

Required
Whey Isolate Protein
2 scoops (~50g) / day in 3pm shake
Use isolate, not concentrate. Faster absorbing, lower lactose. Closes the 185g protein gap without adding excess fat or carbs.
Required
Vitamin D3 + K2
2,000–4,000 IU D3 / 100–200mcg K2 daily
Critical at 60. Low D3 reduces testosterone, impairs muscle protein synthesis, and weakens immune function. Take with a fat-containing meal for absorption.
Required
Magnesium Glycinate
400mg before bed
Most adults over 50 are deficient. Improves sleep quality measurably, reduces cortisol, and supports muscle recovery. Glycinate form — not oxide (low absorption).
Recommended
Creatine Monohydrate
5g/day, any time, no loading
30+ years of research. Preserves muscle in a caloric deficit, improves strength output in calisthenics, and has emerging evidence for cognitive benefits at 60+. Cheapest effective supplement available.
Buy supplements monthly in bulk — 5 lb whey bag, 500g creatine. Do not buy at GNC. Use Thorne (D3/K2, magnesium) and any reputable brand for creatine (it's a commodity). Budget: ~$80–100/month total.

Stall Protocol — In Order

If weight stops moving for 10+ consecutive days, work through these steps in order. Do not skip ahead. Most stalls resolve at step 1 or 2.

1
Add a 20-min post-dinner walk 3 nights per week. Do this for 10 full days before concluding it didn't work. This is a 200–300 cal/day increase in expenditure without touching the diet.
2
Audit actual food intake for 3 days — weigh everything. Eyeballing portions drifts 20–30% high over time. The most common cause of stalls is calorie creep, not metabolic adaptation.
3
Check sleep. Less than 6.5 hours of sleep elevates cortisol, suppresses fat oxidation, and measurably halts fat loss. Magnesium glycinate at 400mg is the first intervention. No screens after 9pm.
4
Add one refeed day per week. Eat at maintenance calories (~2,200–2,400) adding carbs only — not fat or protein. This resets leptin, the hormone that signals your brain to allow fat burning. Do for 2 weeks and reassess.
5
Reassess with advisor only after steps 1–4 have been tried for a minimum of 2 weeks each. Changing the diet is always the last resort, not the first.
What NOT to do when you stall: Cut calories below 1,800. Add more exercise sessions. Skip the shake. These responses feel logical but they accelerate metabolic adaptation, increase muscle loss, and make the stall worse.
